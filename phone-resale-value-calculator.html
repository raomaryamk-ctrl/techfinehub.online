How smartphone depreciation works

A phone is a fast-depreciating asset. The moment it's unboxed it loses "new" status, and value drops steadily from there. As a rough rule, a phone loses 30–50% of its value in the first year, then another 15–25% each year after. Flagship iPhones and Samsung S/Note/Fold models hold value best; mid-range Android phones drop faster; budget phones depreciate fastest of all.

Quick takeawayThe single biggest factors are brand + age. After that, condition, scratches, battery, and PTA status fine-tune the price.

What affects your phone's resale value

  • Brand & model demand — popular, well-known models sell faster and higher.
  • Age — older = cheaper, and very old models lose software support.
  • Condition — screen, body, and corners are inspected first by buyers.
  • Scratches / cracks — a cracked screen can cut value by 30–50%.
  • Battery health — weak battery is a common price-negotiation point.
  • PTA approval, box, warranty — each adds trust and money.

PTA approval and resale in Pakistan

In Pakistan, PTA approval matters a lot. A PTA-approved phone sells for more and faster because the buyer can use any local SIM without blocking or extra tax. A non-PTA phone sells at a discount and to a smaller pool of buyers. Always state the PTA status clearly in your listing — hiding it wastes everyone's time and kills trust.

Be honest about PTANon-PTA isn't unsellable — just price it lower and say so upfront. Surprises at meet-up lose the deal.

Prepare your phone before selling

  1. Clean it thoroughly and remove any dirt from ports and camera.
  2. Find the original box, charger, and cable — they add real value.
  3. Check battery health and note it honestly.
  4. Take clear, well-lit photos from every angle (including any damage).
  5. Back up your data, sign out of all accounts, and factory reset.

Selling on OLX safely

  • Write an honest title with model, storage, and PTA status.
  • Price slightly above your target to leave room for bargaining.
  • Meet buyers in a public place during the day.
  • Accept cash or confirmed transfer only — beware fake screenshots.
  • Never ship before payment; never share OTPs or account details.
