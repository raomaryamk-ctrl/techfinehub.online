How to sell your used phone for the best price

From knowing its real worth to closing the deal safely — a step-by-step guide to getting top value for your old phone.

TFBy the TechFineHub team·7 min read·Updated June 2026

A used phone can be worth a lot more than people assume — if you prep it properly and price it right. Here's how to maximise what you get without the usual hassle.

1. Know its real value first

Before you list anything, find a realistic price range based on brand, age, condition and scratches. Listing blind means you either scare buyers off or leave money on the table.

Get a fair estimate in seconds: Phone Resale Value Calculator →

2. Clean it up — inside and out

Wipe the body, clean the ports gently, and remove any sticky residue. A phone that looks cared-for sells faster and higher. Then back up your data.

3. Factory reset and remove accounts

Sign out of your accounts (this is critical — activation locks tank value and trust), then do a full factory reset. Buyers want a clean, ready-to-use device.

4. Gather the extras

Original box, charger, and especially the receipt or proof of purchase add real value and reassure buyers it isn't stolen.

5. Take honest, well-lit photos

Shoot in daylight from several angles, and show the flaws. Honesty prevents disputes and builds buyer confidence — which lets you hold firm on price.

6. Write a clear, honest listing

State the model, storage, age, battery condition and any scratches plainly. Vague listings attract lowballers; precise ones attract serious buyers.

7. Sell safely

Meet in a public place, verify payment fully before handing over the phone, and never ship before you're paid. Trust your instincts.
